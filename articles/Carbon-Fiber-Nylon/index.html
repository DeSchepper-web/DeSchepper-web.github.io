Carbon Fiber PA6 — Quick Facts

Published: July 25, 2025

Environmental and Health Concerns

  • Main concern: Caprolactam, formerly on EPA's HAP list but removed in 1996. Still a VOC, but not currently of major concern with regards to adverse health effects. Found to be non-carcinogenic in mice. No data available on humans.
  • Regarding other regulated pollutants: PM, O3, CO, SO2, NOx, and Pb — minimal concern
  • Sustainability: Not typically recyclable

Estimated Caprolactam Emissions

  • Emission factor: 1.39 lb/1000 lb PA6-CF (total VOC emission factor, caprolactam would be a percentage of this)
  • Extrusion rate: 25 mm³/s (very high assumption)
  • Caprolactam emitted: 168.9 mg/hour per printer
  • Time to exceed ACGIH TLV in 45 cubic meter room: ~6.1 hours
  • Note: The above calculations represent a highly conservative scenario. In practice, unless you're printing continuously for 24 hours in a very small, unventilated space, indoor printing is likely acceptable. However, using an air curtain, fume hood, or other emission control device is always recommended to minimize exposure.

Quick Facts

  • Max temp: 186 °C
  • Tensile strength: 151 MPa
  • Stiffness: 6 GPa
  • 7x lighter than steel (depends on infill)
  • 2.7x lighter than aluminum
  • Low warping
  • Medium-pressure capable
  • Used in: firearms, drones, auto, functional parts
